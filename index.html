Tuna Cat Treats

  • Time: 30 minutes
Ingredients Quantity
Undrained Tuna 6 ounces
Flour 1 cup
Cornmeal 1 cup
Water 2 Tbsp

Step 1: Preheat oven to 350 degrees.

Step 2: Place all ingredients into mixing bowl, stir well until soft dough forms.

Step 3:Let dough rest for 10 minutes.

Step 4:Cover large surface with cornmeal and roll dough out until ¼ inch thick.

Step 5:Use either a small cookie cutter or knife to cut into shapes.

Step 6:Place on parchment paper lined cookie sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes.

Step 7:Let cool and serve to kitty!

Source: Kitschen Cat

Homemade Churu Treats

  • Time: 10 minutes
Ingredients Quantity
Chicken 4 ounces
Water n/a

Step 1: Measure out 4oz. of chicken (or whichever protein source you are using)

Step 2: Fill pot with enough water to cover the chicken. Bring water to a boil on the stove.

Step 3:Add chicken and let it cook for about 5-6 minutes, or until it is no longer pink in the center.

Step 4:Cut the chicken into small pieces to make things easier on your Ninja or food processor.

Step 5:Move chicken to your Ninja/food processor and add 4 tablespoons of water.

Step 6:Puree the chicken and water together until it reaches a pasty consistency. You don’t want there to be any chicken chunks.

Step 7: Pour the liquid cat treat into the plastic squeeze bottle. I didn’t have a funnel, but if you do, I recommend using it for this step!

Step 8:Cut the tip of the squeeze bottle just to make the hole a little bit bigger.

Step 9:Enjoy!

Source: Kitty Cat Go

Carrot and Catnip Treats

  • Time: 27 minutes
Ingredients Quantity
Olive oil 2 tbsp
Flour 1 1/4 cups
Dried catnip 1 tbsp
Shreded carrot 3/4 cup
Egg 1
Cold water n/a

Step 1:Preheat oven to 375 degrees F.

Step 2:Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

Step 3:In medium bowl add oil and mix in 1 cup of the flour until mixture looks sandy.

Step 4:Stir in catnip and carrots.

Step 5:Mix in egg. Add a little water if the mixture looks too dry and isn't holding together. Add a little flour if the mixture looks too wet and sticky.

Step 6:Sprinkle flat surface with a little additional flour and roll or pat out dough to ¼ inch thickness. Sprinkle dough with additional flour as needed.

Step 7:Prick dough all over with a fork. Use pizza wheel to cut dough into 1/2 inch squares.

Step 8:Arrange squares on prepared baking sheet.

Step 9:Bake for about 12 minutes or until starting to brown.

Step 10:Cool on baking sheet.

Step 11:Store in airtight container for several days or freeze for longer storage. (If baked to a softer texture store in refrigerator.)

Source: Epicuricloud
